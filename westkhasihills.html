WEST KHASI HILLS

West Khasi Hills is a district in the state of Meghalaya, India. It is located in the western part of Meghalaya and is one of the eleven districts in the state. The district is named after the Khasi tribe, one of the major indigenous tribes of Meghalaya, who inhabit the region. The district headquarters of West Khasi Hills is Nongstoin, which is also the largest town in the district. The district shares its borders with Bangladesh to the west and other districts of Meghalaya, including East Khasi Hills and South West Khasi Hills. The terrain of West Khasi Hills is hilly, with rivers, waterfalls, and dense forests. The majority of the population in West Khasi Hills consists of the Khasi tribe, who have their unique language, culture, and traditions. Khasi is the main language spoken in the district, along with English and Hindi. The Khasi people have a rich cultural heritage, including their traditional music, dance, festivals, and crafts. Agriculture is the primary occupation of the people in West Khasi Hills, with cultivation of crops like rice, maize, potatoes, and vegetables being the mainstay of the economy. Livestock rearing, horticulture, and sericulture are also important economic activities in the district. The district is known for its abundant forest resources, including timber, bamboo, and non-timber forest products. West Khasi Hills has several tourist attractions, including natural scenic spots like Nongkhnum Island, Langshiang Falls, and Mawthadraishan Peak. Nongkhnum Island is the biggest river island in Meghalaya, known for its picturesque landscapes and serene surroundings. Langshiang Falls is a spectacular waterfall located in a dense forest, while Mawthadraishan Peak is a prominent peak known for its biodiversity and stunning views. The district also has a rich cultural heritage, with traditional Khasi villages, festivals, and crafts being significant attractions for tourists. The culture and lifestyle of the Khasi people, including their traditional music, dance forms like Shad Suk Mynsiem, and indigenous crafts like bamboo and cane work, are unique experiences for visitors to West Khasi Hills. In terms of infrastructure, West Khasi Hills has a network of roads connecting it to other parts of Meghalaya and neighboring states. The nearest airport is Guwahati Airport in Assam, which is about 200 kilometers from Nongstoin, the district headquarters. Overall, West Khasi Hills is a region with natural beauty, cultural heritage, and unique Khasi tribal culture, known for its tourist attractions, traditional crafts, and indigenous customs.
