SOUTH WEST KHASI HILLS

South West Khasi Hills is a district in the state of Meghalaya, India. It is located in the southwestern part of Meghalaya and is one of the eleven districts in the state. The district is named after the Khasi tribe, one of the major indigenous tribes of Meghalaya, who inhabit the region. The district headquarters of South West Khasi Hills is Mawkyrwat, which is also the largest town in the district. The district shares its borders with Bangladesh to the south and other districts of Meghalaya, including East Khasi Hills and West Khasi Hills. The terrain of South West Khasi Hills is characterized by hills, valleys, rivers, and forests. The majority of the population in South West Khasi Hills consists of the Khasi tribe, who have their unique language, culture, and traditions. Khasi is the main language spoken in the district, along with English and Hindi. The Khasi people have a rich cultural heritage, including their traditional music, dance, festivals, and crafts. Agriculture is the primary occupation of the people in South West Khasi Hills, with cultivation of crops like rice, maize, potatoes, and vegetables being the mainstay of the economy. Livestock rearing, including pig farming, is also an important economic activity in the district. The district is known for its fertile lands and terraced fields, which are a common sight in the hilly terrain of the region. The district is also known for its traditional Khasi villages, where visitors can experience the unique lifestyle, culture, and customs of the Khasi people. The Khasi people are known for their traditional music, dance forms like Ka Shad Mastieh and Shad Suk Mynsiem, and indigenous crafts like bamboo and cane work, which are significant attractions for tourists. In terms of infrastructure, South West Khasi Hills has a network of roads connecting it to other parts of Meghalaya and neighboring states. The nearest airport is Shillong Airport, which is about 85 kilometers from Mawkyrwat, the district headquarters. Overall, South West Khasi Hills is a region with natural beauty, cultural heritage, and unique Khasi tribal culture, known for its tourist attractions, traditional crafts, and indigenous customs.
