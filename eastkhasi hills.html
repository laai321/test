EAST KHASI HILLS

The East Khasi Hills is one of the 11 districts of Meghalaya, a state in northeastern India. It is located in the eastern part of the Khasi Hills region, which is known for its scenic beauty, dense forests, and unique cultural heritage. The district headquarters of East Khasi Hills is Shillong, which is also the capital of Meghalaya. The Khasi Hills are named after the Khasi tribe, which is one of the major tribal communities in Meghalaya. The Khasi people have their own language, customs, and traditions. They primarily practice agriculture, with terraced farming being one of their traditional forms of cultivation. The East Khasi Hills district is known for its rich biodiversity, with several rivers, waterfalls, caves, and forests that are home to diverse flora and fauna. The region is also known for its unique traditional arts, crafts, and cultural festivals, which showcase the rich heritage of the Khasi and other indigenous tribes. Shillong, the capital city of Meghalaya, is located in the East Khasi Hills district and is a major urban center in the state. It is known for its pleasant climate, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is home to various educational institutions, government offices, and commercial establishments, and is a hub of economic and cultural activities in the region. The district is diverse in terms of its population, with people from various ethnicities and communities residing in the region. Apart from agriculture, other economic activities in the district include tourism, trade, and services. However, the challenges of sustainable development, environmental conservation, and preservation of indigenous cultures are important issues faced by the local communities. Efforts are being made to promote sustainable tourism, protect natural resources, and preserve the cultural heritage of the Khasi and other indigenous tribes in the region. The East Khasi Hills district is an important part of the cultural and natural diversity of Meghalaya, and contributes to the overall heritage and identity of the state.
