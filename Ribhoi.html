RI BHOI

Ribhoi district is a district in the state of Meghalaya, India. It is located in the northeastern part of the state and was created on 4th June 1992. The district is named after the River Ribhoi which flows through the region. The district headquarters is located in Nongpoh, which is also the largest town in the district. Ribhoi district is known for its picturesque landscapes, with lush green forests, rolling hills, and rivers. The district is predominantly inhabited by the Khasi tribe, who are known for their unique culture and traditions. The major languages spoken in Ribhoi district are Khasi, English, and Hindi. The economy of Ribhoi district is primarily agrarian, with agriculture being the main occupation of the people. The district is known for its production of various crops like paddy, maize, potato, and vegetables. Livestock rearing and horticulture are also important economic activities in the district. Ribhoi district has several tourist attractions, including natural scenic spots like Umiam Lake, Kyrdemkulai Wildlife Sanctuary, and Lum Nehru Park. These places attract tourists for their natural beauty, biodiversity, and recreational activities like boating, trekking, and picnicking. The district is also known for its traditional arts and crafts, including weaving, basketry, and wood carving, which are integral to the local culture and economy. The unique culture and traditions of the Khasi tribe, including their music, dance, and festivals, are also significant attractions for tourists visiting Ribhoi district. In terms of infrastructure, Ribhoi district has a decent road network, connecting it to other parts of Meghalaya and neighboring states. The nearest airport is Shillong Airport, which is located about 30 kilometers from Nongpoh, the district headquarters. Overall, Ribhoi district is a scenic and culturally rich region in Meghalaya, known for its natural beauty, traditional arts and crafts, and unique Khasi culture.
