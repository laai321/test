EAST GARO HILLS

The East Garo Hills is one of the 11 districts of the state of Meghalaya, located in northeastern India. It is one of the three districts that make up the Garo Hills region of Meghalaya, with the other two being West Garo Hills and South Garo Hills. The district headquarters of East Garo Hills is Williamnagar. The Garo Hills are named after the Garo tribe, which is one of the major tribal communities in Meghalaya. The Garo people have a rich cultural heritage, with their own language, customs, and traditions. They primarily practice agriculture, with jhum or shifting cultivation being one of their traditional forms of farming. The East Garo Hills district is known for its picturesque landscapes, dense forests, and rich biodiversity. The region is endowed with abundant natural resources, including forests, rivers, and wildlife. It is home to several indigenous tribal communities, apart from the Garo tribe, such as the Hajong, Koch, and Rabha tribes, among others. The district is predominantly rural, with agriculture being the main occupation of the local population. Apart from jhum cultivation, other agricultural practices such as terrace farming, wetland cultivation, and horticulture are also practiced in the district. The East Garo Hills are also known for their traditional arts, crafts, and cultural festivals, which showcase the unique heritage of the Garo and other indigenous tribes. Like other parts of Meghalaya, the East Garo Hills district also faces challenges related to development, environmental conservation, and preservation of indigenous cultures. Issues such as deforestation, land rights, and social development continue to be important concerns for the local communities. Overall, the East Garo Hills district is an important part of the cultural and natural diversity of Meghalaya, and it contributes to the rich tapestry of tribal heritage and traditional practices in the state. The district is known for its scenic beauty, cultural richness, and unique way of life of the Garo and other indigenous communities residing in the region.
