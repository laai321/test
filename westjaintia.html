WEST JAINTIA HILLS

West Jaintia Hills is a district in the state of Meghalaya, India. It is located in the western part of Meghalaya and is one of the eleven districts in the state. The district is named after the Jaintia tribe, one of the major indigenous tribes of Meghalaya, who inhabit the region. The district headquarters of West Jaintia Hills is Jowai, which is also the largest town in the district. The district shares its borders with Bangladesh to the south and other districts of Meghalaya, including East Jaintia Hills and Ri-Bhoi. The terrain of West Jaintia Hills is hilly, with rivers, lakes, and dense forests. The majority of the population in West Jaintia Hills consists of the Jaintia tribe, who have their unique language, culture, and traditions. Jaintia is the main language spoken in the district, along with English and Hindi. The Jaintia people have a rich cultural heritage, including their traditional music, dance, festivals, and crafts. Agriculture is the primary occupation of the people in West Jaintia Hills, with cultivation of crops like rice, maize, potatoes, and vegetables being the mainstay of the economy. Livestock rearing, horticulture, and sericulture are also important economic activities in the district. The district is known for its abundant forest resources, including timber, bamboo, and non-timber forest products. West Jaintia Hills has several tourist attractions, including natural scenic spots like Syntu Ksiar, Thadlaskein Lake, and Nartiang Monoliths. Syntu Ksiar, also known as the Krang Suri Waterfall, is a popular tourist spot known for its picturesque waterfall and natural pools. Thadlaskein Lake is a serene lake with beautiful surroundings, and Nartiang Monoliths are ancient stone monoliths that are of historical and cultural significance. The district also has a rich cultural heritage, with traditional Jaintia villages, festivals, and crafts being significant attractions for tourists. The culture and lifestyle of the Jaintia people, including their traditional music, dance forms like Laho, and indigenous crafts like cane and bamboo work, are unique experiences for visitors to West Jaintia Hills. In terms of infrastructure, West Jaintia Hills has a network of roads connecting it to other parts of Meghalaya and neighboring states. The nearest airport is Guwahati Airport in Assam, which is about 150 kilometers from Jowai, the district headquarters. Overall, West Jaintia Hills is a region with natural beauty, cultural heritage, and unique Jaintia tribal culture, known for its tourist attractions, traditional crafts, and indigenous customs.
