NORTH GARO HILLS

The North Garo Hills is one of the 11 districts of Meghalaya, a state in northeastern India. It is located in the northern part of the Garo Hills region, which is known for its rich biodiversity, scenic landscapes, and unique cultural heritage. The district headquarters of North Garo Hills is Resubelpara. The Garo Hills are named after the Garo tribe, which is one of the major tribal communities in Meghalaya. The Garo people have their own language, customs, and traditions. They primarily practice agriculture, with shifting cultivation being one of their traditional forms of cultivation. The North Garo Hills district is known for its diverse flora and fauna, with several rivers, forests, and wildlife sanctuaries that are home to a wide variety of plant and animal species. The region is also known for its unique traditional arts, crafts, and cultural festivals, which showcase the rich heritage of the Garo and other indigenous tribes. The district is predominantly rural, with agriculture being the main occupation of the local population. Apart from shifting cultivation, other agricultural practices such as wetland cultivation, horticulture, and animal husbandry are also practiced in the district. However, the challenges of sustainable agriculture, land management, and natural resource conservation are important issues faced by the local communities. The North Garo Hills district also has a significant tribal population, with various tribes such as the Garos, Hajongs, and Rabhas residing in the region. These tribes have their distinct cultures, languages, and ways of life, which contribute to the rich cultural diversity of the district. The district faces challenges of sustainable development, environmental conservation, and preservation of indigenous cultures. Efforts are being made to promote sustainable livelihoods, protect natural resources, and preserve the cultural heritage of the Garo and other indigenous tribes in the region. In summary, the North Garo Hills district is known for its biodiversity, cultural richness, and unique way of life of the Garo and other indigenous communities residing in the region. It is an important part of the cultural and natural diversity of Meghalaya, and contributes to the overall heritage and identity of the state.
